Detailed Itinerary

Day-by-day breakdown of your adventure

Today you will visit Quebrada de Palala, a spectacular jagged rock formation, formed over many years by intense erosion of the mountainside. After this you will head on to El Sillar where you will have a few minutes to get out and enjoy this dramatic landscape before we continue through Nazarenito, a small gold mining community. Then we will go to visit Ciudad del Encanto, where you will see impressive rock formations. After that, you will arrive to San Pablo de Lípez (4,250m). Then you will go to San Antonio de Lípez and finally to Quetena, where you will spend your first night in a Basic Accommodation.

🚗 ~6–8 hrs driving 📍 Max altitude: 4,250m 🏠 Quetena (basic accommodation)

After breakfast, you will visit your first lagoon, Laguna Kollpa, where large amounts of raw material are extracted for making soap. If you want, you can get out and take a few photographs before continuing through Salar de Chaiviri to arrive at the hot springs in time for a short swim and some lunch. Your next stop after lunch will be Laguna Verde (4,250 m.a.s.l.), which presents an emerald green coloration due to arsenic and magnesium content in its waters. It is located at the feet of Volcano Licancabur (5,868m), which represents the border between Chile and Bolivia. Desierto de Dalí (110 km²) is our next stop, a place with mountains of surrealist colors, reminiscent of Salvador Dalí's paintings and rock formations of petrified lava. Then, we will cross the hot springs once more to go to Sol de Mañana Geysers (5,000m above sea level). Then we will spend around 1 hour in Laguna Colorada. Finally, we will stay close to the lagoon, in Huayllajara.

🚗 ~7–9 hrs driving 📍 Max altitude: 5,000m (geysers) 🏠 Huayllajara (basic accommodation)

After breakfast, you will leave Huayllajara at 07:30 to go to Desierto de Siloli, where we will visit the famous Árbol de Piedra (Stone Tree), one of the most visited tourist attractions in the area. After visiting Árbol de Piedra, we will drive to Laguna Kara and Laguna Kachi, two beautiful Andean lagoons. After that, we will head north and arrive at Laguna Turquiri (Laguna Negra), where you will be able to see black Andean ducks. Finally, we will cross two small towns, Julaca and Alota, to arrive at Candelaria, where we will sleep. At the end of the afternoon, we will enter the Uyuni salt flat to see the sunset, then return to the accommodation.

⚠️ Note: During rainy season (January–April), if the salt flat has a lot of water, we may stay in Uyuni or Colchani instead of Villa Candelaria.
🚗 ~8–10 hrs driving 📍 Max altitude: 4,800m 🏠 Candelaria / Uyuni (basic accommodation)

Today we will cross the immense Salar de Uyuni (3,650 m.a.s.l.) early in the morning to see the Sunrise over the Uyuni Salt Flat. With 12,000 km², the Salar de Uyuni is the largest salt plain in the world, stretching away in all directions as far as the eye can see. From here you can see Volcano Tunupa impressively overhanging. The salt flats are made up of 11 separate layers ranging from 2 to 10m in thickness. The depth of salt reaches 120 meters, making it the biggest reserve of minerals such as lithium, magnesium, potassium, nitrogen, phosphorus and borax in the world. It has 14 small islands; the most important is Isla Incahuasi. Around the island, you can see Ojos de Agua, springs which act as pores for the salt flats. Leaving the salt bed, we will visit Colchani, the village where salt is processed. Here you'll find various salt souvenirs and alpaca/llama wool clothes. Finally, we will go to see the Cementerio de Trenes (Train Cemetery), where ancient trains from the 19th century rest. The tour finishes in Uyuni around 1–2pm.

📌 Optional: Return to Tupiza possible (+3 hrs driving). Ask us when booking.
⚠️ Note: During rainy season, Isla Incahuasi may not be accessible if water levels are high.

What to Bring

  • 🧥 Warm clothes — temperatures drop below 0°C at night at 5,000m altitude
  • 👟 Comfortable walking shoes
  • 🧴 Sunscreen (SPF 50+ recommended — UV is intense at altitude)
  • 🕶️ Sunglasses (UV protection essential)
  • 🧻 Toilet paper (limited facilities en route)
  • 🛁 Towel
  • 🧼 Personal care products
  • 💊 Altitude sickness medication (Soroche pills — available in Tupiza pharmacies)
  • 💵 Cash in Bolivian Pesos (for entrance fees and extras)

Tour Difficulty & Health Information

🟡 MODERATE
  • Max altitude: 5,000m (Geysers Sol de Mañana) — altitude sickness is possible
  • ~6–10 hours of 4x4 driving per day on unpaved roads
  • Walking distances are short (500m–2km per stop)
  • Temperatures range from -10°C (night) to 25°C (midday)
  • Not recommended for people with serious heart or respiratory conditions
  • We carry oxygen tanks for emergencies
  • We recommend consulting your doctor and taking altitude sickness preventive medication
