Day by Day Itinerary

Every day is a new world to discover

1

Your adventure begins in Tupiza, where dramatic red canyons set the stage for an unforgettable journey. First, discover Quebrada de Palala, a spectacular jagged rock formation carved over millennia by wind and rain. Continue to El Sillar, where towering rock spires create a landscape that feels otherworldly. After passing through Nazarenito, a small gold mining community frozen in time, explore Ciudad del Encanto — the 'City of Enchantment' — with its impressive geological formations. As the day unfolds, climb to San Pablo de Lípez (4,250m), then continue through San Antonio de Lípez before arriving at Quetena, where you'll spend your first night under some of the clearest skies you've ever seen.

2

Prepare for the most visually stunning day of the journey. After breakfast, visit Laguna Kollpa before continuing through the Salar de Chaiviri to reach natural hot springs — the perfect spot for a rejuvenating soak at altitude. Be mesmerized by Laguna Verde (4,250m), an emerald jewel at the foot of the imposing Licancabur Volcano (5,868m), marking the border between Chile and Bolivia. Enter the surreal Desierto de Dalí, a landscape of otherworldly colors. Feel the raw power of Earth at the Sol de Mañana Geysers at 5,000m, where steam and mud bubble from deep within. End this incredible day at Laguna Colorada, where thousands of pink flamingos wade through blood-red waters — a sight you'll never forget.

3

Leave Huayllajara at 07:30 and head to the Desierto de Siloli, home to the famous Árbol de Piedra (Stone Tree) — one of Bolivia's most photographed natural wonders, sculpted by centuries of wind erosion. Continue to the beautiful Andean lagoons of Laguna Kara and Laguna Kachi, where reflections create perfect mirrors of the sky. Visit Laguna Turquiri (Laguna Negra), where rare black Andean ducks glide across dark waters. Pass through small Andean towns before arriving at Candelaria. As the day's grand finale, enter the vast Uyuni Salt Flat to witness a breathtaking sunset that paints the endless white landscape in shades of gold and crimson.

During rainy season (January–April), if the salt flat has significant water, the overnight stay may move from Candelaria to Uyuni or Colchani.

4

Wake before dawn to witness one of nature's most spectacular shows: sunrise over the Salar de Uyuni (3,650m). With 12,000 km², this is the largest salt flat on Earth — an infinite white canvas stretching to every horizon, with Volcano Tunupa standing guard in the distance. The salt crust is the world's largest reserve of lithium. Visit Incahuasi Island, a rocky oasis covered in giant cacti surrounded by an ocean of white. Discover the Ojos de Agua, natural springs that bubble through the salt crust. Stop at Colchani, where locals process salt and sell handcrafted souvenirs. End your adventure at the iconic Cementerio de Trenes (Train Cemetery), a haunting collection of rusting locomotives from Bolivia's mining era.

Option to return to Tupiza (additional 3-hour drive). During rainy season, Incahuasi Island may not be accessible.

What to Bring

Pack smart for 4 days in the Bolivian altiplano

Warm clothes Down jacket, thermals, gloves and hat. Nights drop to −25 °C in winter — layers are essential.
Walking shoes Sturdy, closed-toe with good grip. Volcanic rock, salt crust and uneven ground — no sandals.
Sun protection SPF 50+ sunscreen (unavailable on the route) and polarised sunglasses — UV is intense at altitude and the salt flat reflects like a mirror.
Electronics & power Bring a high-capacity power bank — generators shut off at 9 pm. Carry spare camera batteries and keep them warm; cold drains them fast.
Hygiene kit Toilet paper, hand sanitiser, a compact travel towel and personal toiletries. Lodges don't always supply them and running water can be scarce.
Altitude medication The route reaches 5,000 m. Consult your doctor — Diamox or Soroche pills are commonly used. Coca tea also helps.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before booking

The tour reaches altitudes up to 5,000m. We carry oxygen for emergencies and recommend acclimatizing in Tupiza (2,950m) for at least one day before the tour. Drinking coca tea helps, and our guides are trained to handle altitude-related issues.

Temperatures can drop to -15°C at night (May–September) and reach 20°C during the day. During rainy season (January–April), temperatures are milder but some areas may be flooded. Bring layers and warm clothing regardless of the season.

Most accommodations have limited electricity, usually available for a few hours in the evening. We recommend bringing a portable power bank. The Deluxe option hotels have full electricity.

Free cancellation up to 48 hours before departure. Contact us for more details on our refund policy.

Children over 6 years old can join the tour. However, due to high altitude and long driving distances, it's recommended for children over 10 who are comfortable with adventure travel.

We use Toyota Land Cruiser 4x4 vehicles, well-maintained and equipped for the rugged terrain of the Bolivian altiplano.

The Tupiza route includes exclusive landscapes not seen on the Uyuni route: the dramatic red canyons, Ciudad del Encanto, and the southern Lípez region. You also travel in the opposite direction from most groups, meaning fewer crowds at popular stops.

All meals are included: breakfast, lunch, and dinner each day, plus snacks. Our cook prepares fresh meals along the route. Please let us know about dietary requirements in advance.
