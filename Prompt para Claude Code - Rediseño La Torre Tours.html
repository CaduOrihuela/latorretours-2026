Q: What about altitude sickness?
A: The tour reaches altitudes up to 5,000m. We carry oxygen for emergencies and recommend acclimatizing in Tupiza (2,950m) for at least one day before the tour. Drinking coca tea helps, and our guides are trained to handle altitude-related issues.

Q: What is the weather like?
A: Temperatures can drop to -15°C at night (May-September) and reach 20°C during the day. During rainy season (January-April), temperatures are milder but some areas may be flooded. Bring layers and warm clothing regardless of the season.

Q: Is there electricity to charge devices?
A: Most accommodations have limited electricity, usually available for a few hours in the evening. We recommend bringing a portable power bank. The Deluxe option hotels have full electricity.

Q: What is the cancellation policy?
A: Free cancellation up to 48 hours before departure. Contact us for more details on our refund policy.

Q: Is the tour suitable for children?
A: Children over 6 years old can join the tour. However, due to high altitude and long driving distances, it's recommended for children over 10 who are comfortable with adventure travel.

Q: What type of vehicle do you use?
A: We use Toyota Land Cruiser 4x4 vehicles, well-maintained and equipped for the rugged terrain of the Bolivian altiplano.

Q: Why choose the 4-day route from Tupiza instead of the 3-day from Uyuni?
A: The Tupiza route includes exclusive landscapes not seen on the Uyuni route: the dramatic red canyons, Ciudad del Encanto, and the southern Lípez region. You also travel in the opposite direction from most groups, meaning fewer crowds at popular stops.

Q: What meals are included?
A: All meals are included: breakfast, lunch, and dinner each day, plus snacks. Our cook prepares fresh meals along the route. Please let us know about dietary requirements in advance.
